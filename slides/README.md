## Presentation Overview

The slide deck summarises both parts of the project for an academic audience. It covers the motivation, methodology, results, and conclusions across the two tasks.

---

## Slide Structure

### Introduction

- Course context and team members
- Overview of the two-part project structure

### Part 1 — Image Classification (Flickr8k)

- **Dataset overview:** Flickr8k with 5 categories (animals, objects, people, scenes, others)
- **Preprocessing pipeline:** Image resizing (224×224), normalisation, data generators
- **MLP baselines:** Architecture diagrams, performance comparison (MLP 1 vs. MLP 2)
- **CNN architectures:** Appendix CNN, then three optimisation experiments
  - Experiment 1: 2×2 MaxPooling + GlobalAveragePooling
  - Experiment 2: Batch Normalisation
  - Experiment 3: Deeper network + Dropout
- **Data augmentation:** Effect on generalisation
- **Transfer Learning (ResNet50):** Architecture and best results
- **Interpretability:** Saliency maps and Grad-CAM visualisations
- **Summary table:** Validation accuracy across all models

### Part 2 — Time Series Forecasting (London Weather)

- **Dataset overview:** London daily mean temperature (2015–present)
- **Sequence construction:** Sliding window approach and train/val/test split strategy
- **LSTM & GRU:** Architecture diagrams, hyperparameter grid search results
- **1D-CNN:** Comparative architecture and results
- **Model comparison:** Best LSTM vs. GRU vs. CNN on test MSE and MAE
- **Attention mechanism:** Architecture diagram, how attention weights are computed
- **Attention visualisation:** Bar plots of weights over historical timesteps
- **Final comparison:** Baseline RNN vs. Attention RNN vs. CNN

### Conclusions

- Summary of findings across both tasks
- Trade-offs between model complexity and performance
- Value of transfer learning for small image datasets
- Interpretability as a practical benefit of the attention mechanism
- Limitations and potential future directions
